Katsu Curry

Description:

Katsu curry (カツカレー) is a combination of Japanese curry and a crispy cutlet (called katsu in Japanese) coated in panko breadcrumbs all served over steamed Japanese rice.The dish combines crunchy texture with creamy curry, making it perfect for beginners who want to try Japanese cooking at home. It's the ultimate comfort food when you crave a big, hearty meal. At speciality curry restaurants in Japan, you can choose different toppings to go with your curry rice, and katsu is one of them.

When I make curry at home, my boyfriend always prefers to add katsu on top. He just can't get enough of the crunchy katsu texture that pairs perfectly with the rich sauce and slightly sticky Japanese rice. I don't blame him!

Ingredients:

  • 2-4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts (or pork loin/tofu)
  • 1/2 cup plain flour (all-purpose)
  • Vegetable, canola, or neutral oil for frying
  • Salt and black pepper
  • 1 egg
  • 1 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • 2 cups cooked white rice
  • Japanese curry cubes
  • 1 onion
  • 1 carrot
  • 2 cups of water

Steps

  1. Season the chicken with salt and pepper.
  2. Coat chicken in flour, dip in beaten egg, then coat with panko.
  3. Deep fry until golden brown and cooked through.
  4. Cook onion and carrot in a pot until soft.
  5. Add water and curry cubes and simmer until thick.
  6. Slice the chicken cutlet.
  7. Serve over rice and pour curry sauce on top.
